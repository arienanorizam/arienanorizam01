🌾 The Food Security Bioscience Problem

Malaysia aims for 100% self-sufficiency in rice production. Modern high-yield varieties like MR219 grow rapidly in a 120-day cycle. Understanding the peak growth phase is critical for optimal nitrogen fertilizer application and scheduling controlled irrigation drainage.

By modeling cumulative dry matter biomass $f(x)$ (kg/ha) over days $x$, the first-order derivative $f'(x)$ gives the precise Crop Growth Rate (CGR) in kg/ha/day.

📐 Mathematical Model (Logistic Sigmoid)

1. Cumulative Biomass Formula:

$$f(x) = \frac{L}{1 + e^{-k(x - x_0)}}$$

2. Derivative - Crop Growth Rate ($CGR$):

$$f'(x) = \frac{L \cdot k \cdot e^{-k(x - x_0)}}{\left(1 + e^{-k(x - x_0)}\right)^2}$$

Model Parameters (Malaysian MR219 Data):

  • $L$: Max potential biomass limit (e.g., 15,000 kg/ha)
  • $k$: Growth scaling coefficient (0.075 day-1)
  • $x_0$: Inflection point of peak development (Day 60 DAT)
